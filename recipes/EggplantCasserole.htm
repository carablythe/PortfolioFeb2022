Eggplant with Shrimp Casserole (with vegetarian and vegan version)

 

1 small eggplant or half a medium-sized eggplant (slightly more for the vegetarian/vegan version)

1 medium onion, chopped

½ a bell pepper, chopped

¼ C minced parsely

2 cloves of garlic, chopped

 

1 C bread

milk or water  (or soymilk for vegan version)

 

2 t basil (minced fresh, if you have it)

2 t sage (minced fresh, if you have it)

Dash of Tabasco

1 t rosemary

1-2 eggs (or 4 T blended firm tofu for vegan version)

 1 t lemon juice

Tony’s Creole Seasoning or other Cajun seasoning to taste (I put about 1 ½ T)

Dash of red pepper

 

½ C grated Romano cheese (omit for vegan version)

+ ¼ C mixture of grated Romano and other white cheese(s) (omit for vegan version)

 

½ lb. of shrimp, cleaned and deveined (omit for vegetarian/vegan version)

¼ lb. of scallops (optional) (omit for vegetarian/vegan version)

(I haven’t tried this yet, but vegetarians/vegans might want to consider other vegetables like yellow squash, zucchini, and/or mushrooms to replace the seafood in this recipe.)

 

Peel, cut and soak eggplant in salt water for 30 minutes. 

 

Meanwhile, soak bread in milk or water/soymilk, then squeeze. 

 

Blend the eggs (or tofu), basil, sage, Tabasco, rosemary, lemon juice, 1 T of the Cajun seasoning, and red pepper together in a bowl. 

 

In a large bowl, mix this blended concoction with all other ingredients, including the squeezed bread and drained eggplant, BUT RESERVE the ¼ C mixture of cheeses. 

 

Sprinkle in the rest of the Cajun seasoning to taste (about ½ T for me). 

 

Empty the final mixture into a greased casserole pan (9”x9” for a deep casserole).

 

Cover with the remaining cheese. 

 

Bake for about 30-35 minutes at 350º.

 

Adapted from Tony Chachere’s Cajun Country Cookbook, New Revised Edition.
