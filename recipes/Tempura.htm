Tempura    (vegetarian version)

 

Mix together the batter:

1 C sifted flour

1 beaten egg

1 C ice water

Beat lightly together.  The batter should be lumpy, not smooth.

 

Some ideas for seafood and vegetables that are excellent tempura-style:

 

Shrimp – shell and de-vein but leave tails attached (lay shrimp flat and slightly make 3-4 scores across the belly to prevent it from curling when frying)

 

White fresh fish fillet – remove any bones, cut fillets crosswise into bite-sized pieces

 

Squid – cut squid into 1 ½” squares then cross-score the outer, smooth side

 

Green beans – ends trimmed leave whole

 

Carrots – cut diagonally about ¼” thick

 

Sweet potato – peeled, sliced about ¼” thick

 

Zucchini – sliced about ¼” thick

 

White button mushroom caps – halve if caps are large

 

Onion – sliced in half lengthwise then slice ¼” thick  (rings)

 

Okra – whole

 

Eggplant – sliced about ¼” thick

 

Green bell peppers – sliced long-wise, about ½” thick

 

Prepare vegetables and fish and thoroughly dry with paper towel. Heat a significant amount of vegetable oil (enough for deep-frying) in a thick pot or deep fryer at 350 degrees.

 

Place 1 C of flour on a plate, dip vegetables in the flour, and shake off excess.  Then dip in batter and lay in the hot oil and deep fry until golden brown and place on a plate with paper towels to allow excess oil to drain off. 

 

Serve with soy sauce for dipping.  For a spicy kick, add a little wasabi to the soy sauce.
