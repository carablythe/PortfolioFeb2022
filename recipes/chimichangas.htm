Chimichangas

 

Ingredients:

　

Filling:

á    3 cups beef, chicken, shrimp, or veggies (I recommend zucchini, eggplant, bell peppers, corn, spinach, carrots, mushrooms)

á    1 medium onion

á    3 cloves garlic

á    1/2 tsp. salt

á    1/2 tsp. ground cumin

á    1 T olive oil

á    1/4 tsp. cayenne pepper or 1 tsp. diced jalape–os

 

 

Other Ingredients:

10 store-bought or homemade flour tortillas (see recipe)

Topping choices - as desired:

á    shredded white or yellow cheese

á    sour cream

á    shredded lettuce or fresh spinach

á    salsa (see recipe)

 

 

á    guacamole (see recipe)

á    fresh diced tomatoes

á    fresh cilantro

Directions:

1. Blend onion, garlic, and jalape–o in food processor. Add chicken or beef to the food processor with the onion mixture and shred.

2.  Add chosen veggies to the food processor mixture and blend briefly so the veggies are still in chunks.

3.  Put the olive oil in a large frying pan and heat on medium high.

4. Add food processed mixture to frying pan.  Stir in salt, cayenne pepper, and cumin.  Add shrimp - if desired.

5. Cook until the veggies and meat are cooked through - stirring frequently. Season with more salt, cumin, or peppers if needed.

6.  Add 1/2 cup of the filling to center of each uncooked tortilla.  Fold two opposing sides over first and then other opposing sides to completely enclose the filling.

7.  Lay chimichangas folded side down on a greased oven pan.   Sprinkle shredded cheese on top, if desired. Bake at 200 degrees (375 F) for 15-20 minutes until crispy and golden brown.
*** Instead of baking, fry chimichangas in large frying pan with another tablespoon of oil.  Cook until golden brown and crispy on both sides.

8. Serve chimichangas with desired toppings and a side of beans and rice (see recipe).
