Cabbage Potato Soup  (Vegetarian and Vegan)

Serves 4

 

2 C green cabbage – shredded or coarsely chopped

2 diced medium potatoes – red or white, peeled or not

1 small onion, diced

Canned or fresh tomatoes, diced – 3 cups, 1 quart, or 28 oz. can

2 T olive oil

2 t fresh or dried basil

½ -1 t onion salt or powder

½ -1 t coarse black pepper

1 t honey or sugar

Optional (for non-vegetarian/vegan version):  2-4 chicken bouillon cubes

 

 

Sauté potatoes, onion, and cabbage in olive oil on medium high heat for 10-15 minutes – stirring frequently – until onions and cabbage are wilted.  (Add more olive oil if necessary.)  Turn heat to medium, add a cup of water, and stir to release vegetables from bottom of pan.  Add tomatoes, basil, onion salt, and pepper and cook with top on pot for 15 minutes.  Ad honey and taste to see fi more seasonings need to be added.  (Sometimes it needs more sweetening if tomatoes are too acidic).  Turn heat to low, add more water if desired, and simmer covered until potatoes are soft.  (Chicken bouillon cubes can be added now if the taste is not robust enough.) 

 

This is delicious with some French bread or rolls…a good winter soup!!!

 

 

Source:  Donna Phillips
