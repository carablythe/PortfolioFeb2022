The Trick to Making Great Tomato-based Pasta Sauce (vegetarian with vegan version)

 

Begin by sautéing at least 5 cloves of diced garlic (and onions if you like ‘em) in olive oil.  Once they start to turn brown, add your diced veggies of choice.  I really like mushrooms, zucchini and *non-marinated* artichoke hearts in my pasta sauce.  If you do olives, don’t overdo it.

 

Sauté the veggies for a while with fresh or dried basil and a little Italian seasoning.  Let the veggies get a little brown, adding more olive oil if you have to.

 

Use canned pureed or stewed tomatoes + diced fresh tomatoes (red or yellow).  You might even want to add a can of tomato sauce or tomato paste if it is not thick enough.

 

After you add the tomato sauce to the veggies, add more Italian seasoning and Basil to taste.  If you have oregano, add it, too.  If you didn’t add onions, you should add about 1 t of onion powder.

 

NEVER add water.  Use a spatula to get the rest of the tomato sauce out of the cans.

 

Add lots of honey to taste (about 2-3 T) or 1 T sugar (for the vegan version) and at least 1-2 T of basil!  Maybe even throw more garlic in if you like it spicy.

 

Be more generous with the olive oil that you may care to. 

 

You will likely need to add salt. 

 

 

 

What Laurel and I like to do is batter wet zucchini slices and halved mushrooms with a mixture of breadcrumbs and a little Italian seasoning (or even flour, dash of salt, and a little Italian seasoning).  We then bake them on a greased pan, and eat them on top of the pasta.  This is an alternative to putting the zucchini and mushrooms in the pasta sauce.  Yummy!!!
