Norimaki  a.k.a. Sushi Rolls  (with vegetarian/vegan version)

 

Ingredients:

 

1/4 C of sugar

 

3-4 – 1 T  rice vinegar (mild and unflavored is best)

 

1 C of rice (cooked)(sushi rice is best; otherwise, cook white rice a little longer than usual with a little more water to make it stickier)

 

Seaweed (Nori) sheets (I would get mine at East West near Avo's in Ft. Collins, but you might be able to find it at natural foods stores, Asian food stores, or perhaps even the International food section of you local grocery store.)

 

Fish and/or vegetable(s) of choice, cut very thinly (when I use mushrooms and eggplant (skins removed), I usually cook them in a pot with a little bit of soy sauce for about 5- 10 minutes to soften them before wrapping them in the sushi roll).  Avocados, carrots, leaf lettuce, cucumber, and zucchini are good vegetables to use.  For the fish, I would use tuna, halibut (use Pacific…Atlantic is endangered or close to it) or other white fish, salmon (teriyaki’d is a good way), shrimp tempura (see recipe), or whatever fresh salt water fish that you can find and think might be good.

 

Directions:

 

After the rice is cooked (remember, to cook rice it is 1 part rice, 2 parts water in a rice cooker or microwave), let sit while you cook the sugar and vinegar together on high in a pot.  Cook until boiling; take off heat; it should now be a sticky liquid that you can mix into the rice. 

 

Stir for a while until the rice becomes sticky.

 

Place the nori on the rice mat (or other similar device)

Spread the rice over the nori sheet (serrated lines up and in horizontal position).  The rice should be 1/4 inch thick, flush with the left and right edges of the nori sheet and leaving 3/4 inch nori showing on the top and bottom (see illustration)*

 

Place vegetables in the center of the rice, horizontal position *

Then lightly wet bare nori sections with water...and quickly flip bottom edge of mat/device so that the bottom edge of the nori meets the top edge of the nori sheet.  Pull mat/device(with the bulk of the roll under it) with one hand while holding the top edge of the mat with the other hand (that thumb holding down the top edge of the nori sheet.  The bottom edge of the nori should slide from the top edge, down the nori sheet. 

You should now have approximately 1 1/2 inch of nori bare on the top...Now pull the mat away from the roll and roll the roll up to the very top of the nori sheet (should still be a little damp, but if not, lightly dampen so the bare nori will stick to the roll). 

 

Now you should have a complete sushi roll.  Use the mat to tighten the sushi roll in the same manner as before (one hand on the bottom covering the roll, and the other hand on the top, pulling up and away from the roll).  It's okay if some rice and vegetables stick out on the sides of the roll (the edge pieces will just be different that the other pieces). What is important is that the roll stays together and is firm. 

Cut the pieces 1/2 - 1 inch thick. You should use a large (bigger than a steak knife), very sharp, knife.  The cutting is more like sawing quickly.

 

Good luck!! (Ganbatte!)  It is not as difficult as it sounds.  One gets better with practice. I like to mix wasabi (green horse radish...only use a little though, very hot!) in with the soy sauce for dipping.  It is also good to top the pieces with pickled ginger (pink or off-white and you can find them in the Asian market, as well).

 

 

Teriyaki Salmon and Spicy Tuna Roll  (similar to the Jeff Roll)

 

You will use the same approach as with the norimaki.  This is also a norimaki, but with the rice on the outside.  Therefore, you will place the cooked rice (with the sugar and vinegar) on the sushi mat first.  However, it is important to place plastic wrap in between the rice and the sushi mat.

 

Place the nori on top of the flattened, firm square of rice, and pat the nori down with wet fingers. 

 

The filling for this roll is shredded fresh tuna, cooked in a little soy sauce, chili powder, and vegetable/olive oil in a small saucepan or frying pan.  I also like to cook some thinly sliced eggplant with the tuna, and place in the roll with thinly sliced avocado.  Fill generously; this roll is supposed to be rather large.

 

This roll can be loosely rolled, and not as tight as the regular norimaki.  Peel the plastic wrap away and place the roll on a baking sheet, covered in tinfoil, and thoroughly greased.

 

Lather about 1-2 t of mayonnaise on the top of the sushi roll.

 

Place 3-4 teriyaki salmon (see recipe) pieces (about 1/2” thick 2”x2” squares) on top of the roll. Pour some of the teriyaki sauce over the roll.

 

Top off the roll with some finely diced green onions, as desired.
