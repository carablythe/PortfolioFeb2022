African Stew (with vegetarian and vegan versions)

 

Cooking time: 2 hours (1 hour 15 minutes for the vegetarian and vegan versions)

Preparation time: 35 minutes

Main cooking utensil: covered saucepan

 

For 4-5 portions you need:

 

¾ - 1 lb. beef stew meat or chicken (and/or potatoes)

1 medium onion

3-4 large carrots

2 cloves of garlic

2 T tomato paste

1 bay leaf

¼ - ½  t ground cloves

¼ t ground or grated fresh ginger

dash of cayenne pepper

2 t of lemon juice

1/8 – ¼ C of honey  (or for vegan version, sugar, to taste)

¼ C all-purpose flour or cornstarch + water mixture to thicken

2 C water, and keep adding water as it cooks down.

 

  1. Cut the meat (and/or potatoes) into neat pieces, then peel and slice the onions and carrots and crush the cloves of garlic. 
  2. Toss the meat (and/or potatoes) and vegetables in a little vegetable/olive oil for a few minutes
  3. Stir in the tomato paste, bay leaf, spices, and lemon juice.
  4. Bring to a boil, then cover the pan and simmer until tender (for the cooking time above).
  5. Add the flour/cornstarch + water mixture and water, as needed.
  6. Cook, stirring well, for a few minutes until well thickened; taste and re-season if wished.

 

To serve:  with mashed sweet or ordinary potatoes (unless potatoes are already in the stew)
