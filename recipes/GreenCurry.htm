Cara’s Thai Coconut Green Curry (with vegan version)

 

Ingredients:

 

2 T cooking oil

3 gloves of garlic, diced (or 1 Tablespoon garlic powder)

1-inch piece fresh gingerroot, diced matchstick-style

½ medium onion, diced (or 1 Tablespoon onion powder)

1 lb. meat (I either use chicken breast or mixed seafood – shrimp, squid, scallops…omit for vegan version)

1 stalk fresh lemongrass, cut in ½-inch pieces (or equivalent dried lemongrass that has been soaked in water for 30+minutes)

5- 6 kaffir lime leaves (dried okay), ground by hand or with a knive

4 teaspoons Thai green curry paste (check paste ingredients for vegan version)

2 Tablespoons nampla (omit for vegan version)

veggies of choice (eggplant, various colors of bell peppers, mushrooms, broccoli, and diced spinach are my favorites)
1 can unsweetened coconut milk (get the real deal: low-fat version won’t be the same)

1 Tablespoon sugar

1 Tablespoon chicken powder (optional, but really adds flavor. Use veggie stock for vegan version.)

Diced spicy red pepper/red pepper flakes to taste (I put like 1½ T, but I like it spicy!)

Salt to taste (I usually don’t need any after the chicken powder)

 

Cook time 30 minutes (if you have the rice cooking while you are prepping)

 

Preparation:

 

Sauté on high heat in a large pot the diced gingerroot, garlic, onion, and cooking oil for a couple of minutes, sprinkling a few red pepper flakes in the mixture.  Add meat/seafood and stir until half-cooked.  Now add the lemongrass, 1 cup of water (or the dried lemongrass and cup of water it was soaking in), kaffir lime leaves, nampla, and green curry paste.  Stir it a couple of minutes until the curry paste is dissolved.  Bring to medium heat, and add all veggies except broccoli and spinach. Stir occasionally for a few minutes. Pour in the coconut milk.  Put water in the can (another ¾ cup or so) to get the remaining coconut milk; pour into the pot.  Then add sugar, chicken powder, broccoli and spinach.  Stir occasionally for 3-4 more minutes…meanwhile adding pepper flakes and salt to taste.  Now, turn off the heat.  Can sit in the pot for up to 15 minutes before eating.

 

Serve on top of rice. Good with Thai fish cakes and spring rolls as appetizers/side dishes.

 

(NOTE that coconut milk spoils quickly, so eat leftovers within 3 days or freeze.)
