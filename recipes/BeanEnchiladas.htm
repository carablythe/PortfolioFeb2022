White Bean Enchiladas  (vegetarian, currently working on a vegan version)

 

For the Filling:

4 T sour cream (without rennet)

2 (16-ounce) cans cannelloni beans or other white beans, rinsed and drained

1 C  (4 ounces) shredded Mexican blend or combined cheddar and white cheeses

4 T canned green chiles

2 T sliced green onions

1 T fresh cilantro

2 t ground cumin

 

For the Sauce:

1 (10-ounce) can enchilada sauce (such as Old El Paso or Ortega)

2 (8-ounce) cans of tomato sauce

2 t chili powder

 

12 (6-inch) corn tortillas

a little cheese (3-4 T) to cover the enchiladas later

½ C sour cream as a topping

 

Mix the filling ingredients in a blender or food processor (preferably).  Fill the corn tortillas with this blended mixture and roll them, placing them side-by-side in a 13”x9” greased pan.  Mix the sauce ingredients in a bowl, and then pour over the enchiladas evenly.  Sprinkle the remaining cheese on top, and top each enchilada with about 2 small spoonfuls of sour cream.  Bake at 350˚ for 15-20 minutes or until bubbly and cheese is browning.
