Greek Style Crab Cakes

 

INGREDIENTS:

 

1/2 a large leek, cut in a few pieces
2 Tbsp. Olive Oil

1/3 Cup Finely Minced Celery

½ Cup Fresh Mint, Finely Chopped

1 tsp. ground dill
1 ½ Teaspoons Dried Oregano
3 cloves minced garlic
½ Teaspoon Salt
¼ Teaspoon Black Pepper
1 T lemon juice
½ Cup Yogurt
1 Egg
450g Crab Meat (canned or fresh)　
3 1/2 Cups Panko Breadcrumbs ( 2 Cups For Crab Mix and 1 1/2 Cup For Coating Cakes)
¼ cup of  Butter or Margarine

1 lemon, cut into wedges  

DIRECTIONS:

 

1.     In a food processor combine olive oil, leek, Celery, Mint, Dill, Oregano, Garlic, Salt, Pepper, Lemon Juice, and Egg.  Blend well.

2.     Pour the mixture into a large mixing bowl. Carefully add the crabmeat, yogurt and breadcrumbs to the mixture.  Gently fold the ingredients all together. 

3.     Pour the remaining breadcrumbs on a large plate or cookie sheet.  Using your hands (covered in olive oil), take a fistful or large spoonful (the size you like) of the mixture and form into a patty.  Place patty on breadcrumb bed and lay the patty on the breadcrumbs, sprinkle breadcrumbs on top as well, so top and bottom are coated. 

4.     Place prepared crab cake on greased baking sheet.  (Repeat the process with all of the mixture.)

5.     Put the crab cakes in the preheated oven at 200 degrees for 10 minutes. Take out to flip.  Then put them in for another 10 minutes. Set aside to cool.

6.     Place a lemon wedge beside or squeeze onto the crab cake and serve with a tzatziki sauce.

 

Adapted from: Antigoni Sander McCloud, owner of Kafe 421
