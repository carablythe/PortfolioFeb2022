Apple Dumplings      (Vegan)

 

Serves 12

For the pastry:

1 C vegetable shortening/margarine
3 C flour
1 ½  t salt
2/3C cold water

 

For the apples:

12 medium-sized tart apples, such as Granny Smith or Jonathan
3 T raisins soaked in 3 T rum (preferably at least 1 hour of soaking)

3T oatmeal (uncooked)
1 t margarine per apple (4 T)

 

For the syrup:

2 ½ C firmly packed brown sugar
2 ½ - 3 C water
1 stick margarine

any apple pieces leftover from coring

Serve hot or at room temperature.  GREAT with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream, but also yummy just like it is. J

 

1. To make the pastry, see the Pie Crust recipe. Refrigerate until ready to use.

 

2. Now, peel (if desired) and core the apples.

 

3. Divide the pastry into 12 parts. Roll each part (with a roller on a floured surface) into a circle large enough to cover the apple. Set each on a greased baking sheet. Place the apple in the center, and bring up the sides to encase it, but don’t close it yet.

 

4. Place ¼ teaspoon of oatmeal in each apple core hole. Then add 1 teaspoon of margarine. Divide the rum-soaked raisins and any remaining rum into the holes on top of the butter. Pinch the top of the pastry together.

 

5. Preheat the oven to 450°F.  Combine the syrup ingredients in a medium saucepan on the stove, and bring to a boil. Simmer for 3 minutes (occasionally stirring).

 

6.  Pour about 2 T of syrup over each covered apple. Place them in the oven and bake for 10 minutes. Reduce the heat to 330˚F, and pour more syrup over the apples. Bake an additional 20 minutes, pouring syrup every 10 minutes. Remove from the oven and allow to cool for 5 minutes. Pour the remaining syrup into a large glass dish/corning ware.  Place apples and any “gooey” juice from the baking sheet into this container.

 

Note: The pastry and syrup can be prepared up to 2 days in advance and refrigerated. The apples should be peeled just prior to baking.

 

Adapted from the recipe of:  Christiana Campbell's Tavern, Williamsburg, Virginia
