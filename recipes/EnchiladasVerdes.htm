Enchiladas Verdes   (with Vegetarian version…no vegan version available)

 

12 (6-inch) corn tortillas

 

For the Filling:

4 C finely chopped chicken breast (about 1 ½ lb.), sautéed with a little bit of minced garlic, ground cumin, and chili powder.

OR, for the vegetarian version, use about 4 C sliced mushrooms (portabella is best), tofu, peeled and sliced eggplant…sautéing the veggies/tofu the same way as you would the chicken…and/or just more cheese.

1 ½ C shredded white cheddar, Monterey Jack or Mexican blend cheese

1 small (5-ounce?) can diced green chiles

 

For the Sauce:

2-3 (10-ounce) cans or one (28-ounce) can Green Chile Enchilada Sauce (like Rosarita)

½ C dice onions

4 garlic gloves, minced

 

OR make your own sauce:

 

12 long green chiles or Anaheim chiles (about 2 pounds)

2 C diced onion

1 ½ C chopped tomato

½ C water (add more later if needed)

1 t salt

1 t ground cumin

7-8 garlic gloves, minced

 

  1. Preheat oven to 450˚.
  2. Place chiles on a greased foil-lined baking sheet, bake at 450˚ for 20 minutes or until blackened, turning one.  Place chiles in a ziplock bag, seal and let stand 10 minutes.  Peel, discard seeds and membranes.  Cut chiles into thin strips.  Place chiles and remaining ingredients in a medium saucepan.  Bring to a boil, cover, reduce heat, and simmer 20 minutes. 

 

For the topping:

¾ C combined shredded cheddar and Monterey Jack/white cheddar cheese

½ C sour cream (optional)

 

 

Put the cheese, green chiles, and cooked chicken into the corn tortillas and roll, placing them side-by-side in a 13”x9” greased pan.  Pour the sauce (after you mix the ingredients together and cook if you are making it from scratch) over the enchiladas evenly.  Sprinkle the remaining cheese on top, and, if you wish, top each enchilada with about 2 small spoonfuls of sour cream.  Bake at 350˚ for 15-20 minutes or until bubbly and cheese is browning.
