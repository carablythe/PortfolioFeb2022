Vegan Brownies

 

8 oz. Silken Tofu, drained

1 ½ cups plus 6 T unbleached white flour

2/3 C water

1 ½ C sugar

1 t salt

1 t vanilla extract

¾ C unsweetened cocoa

½ C vegetable oil

1 t baking powder

(I also like to add vegan chocolate chips, about 1 C)

 

Silken tofu is sold in 10.5 oz. Aseptic boxes.  It can be found in most grocery stores in the produce section.  I use firm tofu for this recipe.

 

This recipe has a lot of steps and it is tempting to take shortcuts, but I’ve tried this and I’ve never gotten the same great taste and texture as with the original method.

 

Preheat oven t o350 degrees.  Grease and flour a 9”x9” baking pan.  In a food processor or blender, puree tofu until smooth. 

 

In a small saucepan, mix 1/3 C of the flour with the water until smooth.  Stir in pureed tofu until blended.  Cook over low heat, stirring occasionally, until heated through (about 5 minutes).  Transfer to medium bowl and allow to cool completely.

 

Add sugar, salt and vanilla to tofu mixture, and mix well. 

 

In a small bowl, mix cocoa and oil, then stir into tofu mixture.  Stir in remaining 1 ½ C of flour and the baking powder until blended.  (Here’s where I add the chocolate chips.)

 

Scrape mixture into prepared pan, smoothing the top.  Bake until top is dry and springs back when gently pressed, about 25 minutes.  Set pan on wire rack to cool, then cut into bars.
