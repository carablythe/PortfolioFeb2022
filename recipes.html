Caramel Apples

Ingredients:

8 apples, any variety, washed and dried

1 cup heavy cream

1/4 cup light corn syrup (Karo)

1/2 cup honey

1/2 cup (1 stick) butter or margarine

1 cup sugar

1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Directions:

1. Insert a stick all the way into the stem end of each apple.

2. Line a large baking pan with parchment (cooking) paper.

3. Fit a large saucepan with a candy thermometer. Over high heat, cook 3/4 cup of the cream, the corn syrup, honey, butter, and sugar to 118 degrees C (246 degrees F), or until soft to firm ball stage; at this point the syrup will be golden.

4. Remove from the heat and carefully swirl in the remaining cream and the vanilla. Use caution; this is very hot and it may splatter.

5. While the caramel is hot, dip and turn the apples into the caramel to coat and let the excess drip off. You can use a ladle to pour the caramel over the apples, if easier.

6. Arrange the apples on the cookie sheet and let cool.

Ginger Cookies

Dry Ingredients:

2 cups flour

1/2 tsp. salt

1 tsp. cloves

1 tsp. ginger

1 tsp. cinnamon

3 tsp. baking soda

1 cup sugar

Wet Ingredients:

3/4 cup vegetable shortening

1 egg

1/4 cup molasses

Directions:

1. Mix together the dry and wet ingredients in separate bowls.

2. Then slowly add the two mixtures together.

3. Roll into balls, and roll the balls around in a plate of sugar.

4. Set the balls a couple of inches apart on a greased or lined cookie sheet.

5. Bake at 180 degrees C (350 degrees F) for 8-10 minutes.

Brownies NEW! Watch Brownie Cooking Demo Here!

Ingredients:

3/4 cup vegetable oil

1 1/2 cup white sugar

2 teaspoon vanilla extract

2 eggs

1 pack (150g) tofu

1 1/2 cup all-purpose flour

3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder

1/2 teaspoon baking powder

1/4 teaspoon salt

1 cup chocolate chips

1/2 cup chopped walnuts and/or 1/2 cups melted cream cheese (optional)

Directions:

1. Preheat oven to 180 degrees C (350 degrees F). Grease a baking pan.

2. Mix all ingredients except the nuts and cream cheese in the large mixing bowl.

3. Then, pour the mix into the greased pan.

4. add the walnuts and/or cream cheese and swirl in.

5. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes, or until the brownie begins to pull away from edges of pan.

6. Let cool on a wire rack before cutting into squares.
